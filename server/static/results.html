Case 4 stresses recency / training-cutoff honesty: GLP-1 evidence, FDA decisions, employer-benefit guidance, and rebate dynamics have all moved fast since most models' training cutoffs. The headline finding: the local council fabricated citations

Three concrete confabulations in the local-council Healthcare seat:

  • "SEMIMAN trial" — does not exist. The semaglutide obesity pivotal trial is STEP 1 (Wilding et al., NEJM 2021).
  • "Marso SP et al. … STEP" — STEP 1 was authored by Wilding et al. Steven Marso is the lead author of SUSTAIN-6 (the diabetes outcomes trial). Wrong author attached to the right trial.
  • "Armstrong M et al. … SURMOUNT-1" — SURMOUNT-1 was authored by Jastreboff et al., NEJM 2022. Armstrong didn't run SURMOUNT-1.

All three are confidently presented in a "References" footer block with NEJM/Lancet journal-style formatting that mimics a peer-reviewed citation list. This is the worst possible failure mode for a domain fine-tune — the model is supposed to be more reliable on medical literature than a generalist, and is instead producing confident, plausible-shaped fabrications.

Analysis — Cross-border digital therapeutic launch

Case 2 stresses jurisdictional vocabulary discipline: a US-cleared SaMD launching simultaneously in the UK and Germany has to keep multiple regulatory regimes distinct (MHRA vs BfArM, UK GDPR vs EU GDPR, UK MDR 2002 vs EU MDR 2017/745, DiGA pathway vs NHS commissioning). This is the case explicitly designed to be hard on jurisdictional precision.

The local council missed the central pathway

Five specific failures in the local council, in a case designed to test exactly these things:

  • DiGA — the Digitale Gesundheitsanwendung framework — is never mentioned. This is the central prescription-DTx reimbursement pathway in Germany. opus-single spent half its volume on DiGA mechanics (pVE evidence, §139e SGB V, BfArM provisional vs permanent listing, GKV-SV negotiation). The local council didn't surface its existence.
  • UK GDPR and EU GDPR are treated as one regime. Saul calls the Data Protection Act 2018 "based on GDPR" — post-Brexit divergence (the textbook jurisdictional-vocabulary test) is invisible.
  • Saul cites MDD and IVDD as live regulation. The MDD was replaced by MDR in May 2021, and IVDD by IVDR in May 2022. Listing all three as concurrent live frameworks is wrong for 2026.
  • The Healthcare seat misreports its own training cutoff. Quote: "trained up to 2021." Llama 3.1 (Med42's backbone) has data through late 2023. This false epistemic humility justifies vague disclaiming of developments that fall within the model's actual training window.
  • The Finance seat invents entities. "Digital Therapeutics Accelerator" (not a real NHS body), "Digital Therapeutics Association" framed as a German guideline-setter (DTA is a US industry trade group), "NHS Business Services Authority evaluates products" (BSA handles payments, not evaluation — NICE/ICBs do that).

Where opus-council pulled ahead of opus-single

Three things in the opus-council output that the opus-single integration didn't catch — load-bearing for the article's "what does seat isolation buy you" question:

  • §34(1) Nr. 4 SGB V with the 2022 narrow exception for severe tobacco dependence (ICD-10 F17.2) allowing two reimbursed pharmacotherapy attempts within three years. This is a micro-statutory detail very few people in the field would surface unprompted.
  • The §34 × adjunct trial design tension. Healthcare independently recommended an "adjunctive" trial design; Legal/Finance separately observed that adjunct pharmacotherapy is largely out-of-pocket for the reimbursed German cohort. A single mind writing all four roles probably smooths past that contradiction — the orchestrated mode's seat isolation surfaces it.
  • The AI Act × MDR product-design decision. Adaptive ML personalization triggers high-risk AI Act stack under Article 6(1) — correctly framed as a design decision that has to be made before clinical- investigation protocol lock, not as a downstream compliance afterthought.

Pattern across both cases

Two cases, two failure modes (recency honesty + jurisdictional vocabulary), one consistent pattern. Local council produces structurally-correct but substantively-thin output with specific confabulations and false-cutoff disclaiming. Both Opus modes hit every rubric must-have. opus-council does slightly more than opus-single: more case law, more statute pin-cites, and the orchestrated mode's seat-isolation produces cross-domain tensions (case 2's §34 × adjunct trial design; case 4's CAA fiduciary × rebate-driven formulary) that a single mind likely smooths past.

The article frame this points to is not "specialization beats frontier" but rather: architecture preserved, fine-tunes underperformed, frontier dominated even without architectural scaffolding, and orchestration adds value at frontier scale via seat isolation forcing tension extraction.
